01

The launch menu — keep it tiny on purpose

Five items only. The #1 mistake first-time kitchens make is launching with 25 items and dying of spoilage and mis-picks in week one.

  • Chicken curry — bone-in, “ghar jaisa”. Our hero, highest volume.
  • Mutton curry — premium hero, weekend driver.
  • Plain rice — cheap to make, high margin.
  • Chapati — make-to-order. (Curry with no bread loses ~40% of orders in this area — this is why we added it.)
  • Salad — small garnish cup. Lifts perceived value for ~₹10 cost.

What we are deliberately NOT launching with

No biryani, no starters, no veg gravies, no egg. Add a free ₹6 dal/raita add-on only in month 2, once order data shows what sells. Clean and simple wins the first 90 days.

Price for two — what Swiggy & Zomato ask

We launch à-la-carte (no combos — keep it simple). The 4-piece curry already serves 2. For the platforms’ “cost for two” figure — a full meal for two with rice, chapati & salad — use these references:

Full meal for twoSwiggy/Zomato
Chicken (4-pc curry + rice + chapati + salad)≈ ₹549
Mutton (4-pc curry + rice + chapati + salad)≈ ₹1,149

Set the “Cost for two” shown on your Swiggy/Zomato profile to ≈ ₹550 (chicken is the volume driver; mutton meals run higher).

The QR move — convert aggregator customers to direct

Every parcel carries a brand sticker with a WhatsApp QR. A customer who paid ₹329 on Swiggy scans it and reorders direct at ₹270 next time — we make more, they pay less, and the platform is cut out. Build the direct channel from day one.

04

Why the menu got rejected — and how we fix it

Rejections are almost never about prices being “bad” — they’re mechanical. Fix all six:

  • No GSTIN yet = auto-reject. GST is filed (~7 days out). Do not re-submit the menu until the GSTIN is in hand and mapped to the restaurant. This is the #1 cause.
  • Every item needs a “serves” + portion. A bare “Chicken Curry ₹120” with no size gets flagged as incomplete. Our 2-pcs / 4-pcs + serves-1 / serves-2 labels fix this.
  • Keep main dishes realistically priced. A suspiciously low main dish price can trip the platform’s “data error” filter — our curries start at ₹169. Small add-ons like chapati (₹15) and salad (₹39) being cheap is normal and fine.
  • GST category must be “Restaurant — 5%, no input credit.” Wrong tax mapping = reject. Never tick 0% or 18%.
  • Real, well-lit photos. Use our 1024×1024 food JPGs. Zomato rejects dark / stock images.
  • Fuller menu clears faster. Add a one-line description + a photo per item. A bare item list with no descriptions reads as “incomplete”.

Per-order packaging cost lands at ₹12–18 (single dish) to ₹35–40 (large 4-pc + sides order) — already included in the prices above.

Three rules that prevent 90% of packaging disasters

1. Curry/gravy → always leak-proof PP snap-lid, never plain aluminium foil. Foil seams leak gravy in the bag = guaranteed 1-star review. Foil is only for dry rice/chapati.

2. Buy ONE container family with a shared mouth diameter (250/350/500/750) so a single lid fits all — cheaper, no wrong-lid scramble in the dinner rush.

3. Every parcel = container + lid + spoon + napkin + tamper sticker over the lid. Aggregator audits and customers both check the seal.

Dry / masala stock — buy monthly, wholesale (K R Market)

  • Staples: Sona Masoori rice (25 kg), atta (10–15 kg from chakki), sunflower oil (15 L tin), ghee (1–2 kg), salt, sugar.
  • Masala: turmeric, red chilli powder, coriander powder, jeera (cumin), garam masala, black pepper, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, bay leaf, mustard seeds, kasuri methi. (Ginger-garlic paste = make fresh daily, don’t buy bottled.)

Golden rules for Bangalore heat — read once, follow daily

1. Onion / garlic / ginger → NEVER in the fridge. Fridge moisture rots them. Keep in an open ventilated basket, cool & dark, off the floor. Stored dry, onion keeps 1–2 weeks — so buying a week’s worth is safe. The damage you fear comes from moisture + plastic bags + piling up, not from quantity.

2. Never store onion next to potato — they spoil each other. (We don’t use potato much, but remember it.)

3. Leafy greens: don’t wash before storing. Pick out any rotten leaves, wrap dry in newspaper/cloth, keep in the crisper. Washed-then-stored = slimy in a day.

4. Tomato: buy firm, not fully red. Soft ripe tomatoes turn in 2 days in our heat. Buy a touch under-ripe.

5. Raw meat lives on the BOTTOM shelf in a sealed box so it can’t drip onto veg — and is used within 24 h. One cross-contamination = a food-poisoning review that sinks the rating.
