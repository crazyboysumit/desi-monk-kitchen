05

Step 5 — Reality check & ramp plan

Be honest: 81 orders/day is a lot for one home kitchen with 2 cooks

~80 orders/day (16 kg chicken + 9 kg mutton daily, cooked fresh twice a day) is near the ceiling of what a head cook + one assistant can produce with quality. Pushing volume without help = late orders, bad ratings, burnout. Plan to add a third cook / one helper once you cross ~45–50 orders/day (that adds ~₹15–18k to fixed cost, which only lifts the target a little).

Realistic 90-day ramp

Month 1: reach break-even (~22 orders/day). Month 2: push to ~₹1L (~50/day) as Swiggy/Zomato ranking + repeat WhatsApp orders build. Month 3+: add a helper and drive toward ₹2L (~81/day). The ₹2L goal is realistic by month 4–6, not week one.

06

Step 6 — Five levers that get you to ₹2L faster (without more orders)

  • Shift orders to DIRECT (WhatsApp/Google). Every order that comes direct instead of via Swiggy/Zomato earns ~₹20–30 more (no 28% cut). Move 55%→30% aggregator and the same orders make far more profit. The QR sticker on every parcel is how.
  • Raise the average bill. Always offer rice + chapati + salad with every curry. Going from ₹420 to ₹500 average bill is ~20% more profit on the same order count.
  • Push mutton & 4-pc portions. A family mutton order makes ₹226–254 vs ₹80 for a single chicken — nudge customers up with combos/photos.
  • Cut food cost 5%. Lock a fixed daily chicken/mutton rate, buy rice/spices wholesale. 5% off meat ≈ ₹8–10k/month straight to profit.
  • Kill wastage. Cook to forecast; spoilage of meat/veg is pure loss. Even 5% wastage at the ₹2L scale is ~₹15–20k/month gone.

Bottom line

Cover costs at ~22 orders/day. Make ₹1L at ~51/day. Hit the ₹2L two-partner goal at ~81 orders/day (~₹8.8L sales/month) — best reached by month 4–6 with a helper and a strong direct-order channel.
