Daily operations

The actual workflow — what to do each day, when, and in what order. Get this watertight before scaling marketing.

Marination & prep — the core question

You asked: should we marinate in the evening for next-day, or marinate in the morning at 5–6 AM? Here's the food-science answer:

ItemMinimum marinationBest (flavour)Practical plan
Chicken (curry / kadai / butter)2 hr6–8 hrMarinate previous night 9–10 PM after dinner service. Cook from 10 AM next day.
Mutton (curry / rogan)4 hr12–24 hrMarinate previous night 8 PM. Mutton without long marination = chewy. Pressure-cook 10 AM.
Rice (jeera / plain)30 min soak30 min soakSoak fresh every meal. Don't pre-cook the night before — texture suffers.
Dal tadka——Cook fresh in the morning. Tadka just before packing.
Chapati / naan dough20 min rest40 min restMix dough at 10:30 AM. Roll & cook to order — never pre-roll.
Raita——Whisk fresh that morning; refrigerate in 100 ml cups till order.

Cold-chain rule

Marinated raw meat must stay below 4 °C. Buy a small fridge thermometer (₹250). If power goes out for > 2 hours, throw it. One food-poisoning incident on Zomato = your rating crashes from 4.2 to 2.8 in three days and you don't recover.

Menu & SKUs

Keep the menu small. The biggest mistake first-time cloud kitchens make is launching with 25 items and dying of inventory complexity.

Rebel Foods / Box8 lesson

Both started with < 8 SKUs and grew only after data showed which sold. With 5 SKUs you can hold ingredient inventory tight; with 25 you waste 30 % to spoilage in week one.

Combo design rules

  • Every combo must contain one carb (rice or chapati), one protein, one wet (raita / dal), and one tiny garnish (salad / papad / pickle). Even ₹5-cost garnish lifts perceived value 30 %.
  • Family packs: keep components separate in the box (4-compartment thali style). Mixing rice + curry kills shelf life inside 30 min.
  • Don't offer too many add-ons on aggregators — every checkbox is one more thing to mis-pick during dinner rush.

05

Pricing & cost

This section will save or sink the business. Read it slowly.

The aggregator commission trap

Swiggy and Zomato take 22 – 26 % commission on the order value plus 18 % GST on commission. Add payment gateway (~2 %) and offer share (often forced). Net loss to platform: ≈ 28 %.

If you list at ₹120, the platform pays you back ₹120 × (1 − 0.28) = ≈ ₹86.

If your cost-of-goods is ₹90 and packaging is ₹10, you lose ₹14 per order. Marketing it more = bleeding faster.

Two prices, one menu

The fix is aggregator menu price ≠ direct menu price. Almost every successful cloud kitchen does this. Most aggregators allow it; some flag it but don't enforce.

ChannelListed price (chicken meal)You receiveCostProfit
Swiggy / Zomato₹185₹133₹100+₹33
Direct WhatsApp / Instagram₹150₹150₹100+₹50
Walk-in / pickup₹140₹140₹100+₹40

Same plate, three prices. The customer paying ₹185 on Swiggy is paying for the convenience and the platform's marketing. The customer ordering directly gets a better deal — and reorders without the platform middle-manning.

Discount / launch-offer rules

  • The "30 % off first 6 months" idea is fine only on direct channels. On aggregators that becomes free food for them.
  • On Swiggy / Zomato, use their own "Restaurant Funded Offer" tool sparingly — never > 20 %.
  • Better than discount: add a free raita or free dal cup. Costs you ₹6, customer values it at ₹40.

Chicken sourcing — practical tips

  • 1 kg dressed chicken (skinless, with bone) = 4 quarter portions of 180–200 g cooked weight.
  • Fresh > frozen. Frozen chicken loses 12–15 % weight on thaw and tastes flat.
  • Negotiate per-kg rate at fixed quantity. Ex: "I'll pick 8 kg every morning at 6 AM, give me ₹250/kg flat." Local shops always say yes for guaranteed daily volume.
  • Always buy 10 % more than forecast — running out at 2 PM = lost orders + bad reviews.

Mutton sourcing

  • Sunday is mutton day in Bengaluru — mandi prices spike. Buy Saturday evening, marinate overnight, cook Sunday morning.
  • Always have 2 butchers on rotation. One will let you down at the worst possible time.
  • Ask for "front leg + shoulder" mix — best gravy meat. Avoid pure ribs unless customer specifies.

08

Swiggy & Zomato onboarding

7–14 days each from application to live menu. Don't wait for 100 % perfection — get applications in the moment GST is approved.

Documents you'll be asked for

Swiggy partner — onboarding flow

  1. Apply at partner.swiggy.com. Fill business details using GSTIN address.
  2. Upload all docs above. Cuisine: North Indian / Mughlai. Service type: Cloud kitchen.
  3. Wait 3–5 days for first call-back from onboarding manager. Save their direct phone — you'll need them later for menu changes.
  4. Sign digital agreement. Commission starts at 22 %, will rise to 24–26 % after first promo participation.
  5. Build menu in their dashboard. Each item: name, description (≤ 150 chars), price, photo, in-stock toggle, prep-time.
  6. Switch shop to "Live" — first orders trickle in within 24 hr.

Zomato partner — onboarding flow

  1. Apply at zomato.com/partner-with-us.
  2. Same docs as Swiggy. Add menu via Zomato Restaurant Partner app.
  3. Zomato is stricter on photos — they'll reject low-light shots. Re-shoot if asked.
  4. Commission: starts ~22 %, can hit 28 % with promos.
  5. Once live, immediately enable: Pre-order, Free delivery sub-zone, Auto-accept orders.

The "Hyperpure" question

Zomato will pitch you Hyperpure (their B2B supply arm) — chicken, oil, packaging at "competitive prices". Reality: rates are 5–10 % above local mandi for chicken. Useful only for stuff you can't find locally (specific cheese, frozen items). Don't migrate everything to Hyperpure — vendor lock-in.

09

Direct ordering channels

These are the channels where you keep 100 % of the price. Build them from day one — by month 3 they should be 30–40 % of orders.

WhatsApp Business — your single most important channel

  1. Install WhatsApp Business on the kitchen phone (separate number from personal).
  2. Set up Catalog: every menu item with a 1024 × 1024 photo, name, price.
  3. Set Greeting message, Away message, Quick replies ("Order placed", "ETA 30 min", "Out for delivery").
  4. Generate a https://wa.me/91XXXXXXXXXX?text=Hi short link → put it on the website, every paper bag QR sticker, every Instagram bio.
  5. Use Labels: "New", "Repeat", "Family-pack regular", "Refunded" — these become your CRM.

Instagram — slow burn, brand building

  • Bio: "Home-cooked North Indian, Bommanahalli · 12–15:30 / 19–23 · WhatsApp →" + the wa.me link.
  • Post 4× per week: 2 dishes + 1 behind-the-scenes (Babita cooking) + 1 customer review. Reels > static photos.
  • Hashtags: localised — #bommanahalli #silkboard #btmlayout #bangalorefoodies #homefoodbangalore.
  • Run a tiny Meta ad: ₹500/day, click-to-WhatsApp, audience = Bengaluru + 3 km of Bommanahalli, age 25–45. Optimise for "Engagement" first 2 weeks; switch to "Messages" once posts are warm.

Google Business Profile — most-skipped, highest ROI

  • Free. Claim your kitchen on business.google.com.
  • Verification: by post (5–7 days) or video. Use video — instant.
  • Post photos, hours, phone, WhatsApp link. People searching "home food near me" or "tiffin Bommanahalli" find you free.

Pamphlets / society distribution

  • 1,000 A5 colour pamphlets ≈ ₹1,500–2,000. Print menu (8 SKUs max, photo per item) + WhatsApp QR + 10 % off first order coupon code.
  • Distribution: pay a young helper ₹600/day to slip pamphlets under apartment doors in Sakshi Heights, Brigade, etc., 6–8 PM (before dinner).
  • Use a unique coupon code per pamphlet batch ("PAMPH1") so you can measure ROI in WhatsApp orders.

10

Marketing plan — 30 / 60 / 90 days

A simple month-by-month sequence so you don't burn cash on the wrong channel at the wrong time.

Month 1 — survive & learn

  • Goal: 5 orders/day average, ratings ≥ 4.2 on both apps, zero food-poisoning complaints.
  • Budget: ₹3,000 — pamphlets only. No paid digital yet.
  • Why no ads: aggregator algorithm will boost any new restaurant for 30 days regardless. Don't spend on top of free boost.
  • What you watch every night: rating, % of 5★ reviews, average prep time, # of cancellations.

Month 2 — turn on the funnel

  • Goal: 10–15 orders/day, ≥ 30 % from direct channels.
  • Budget: ₹6,000 — Meta click-to-WhatsApp ad ₹500/day × 8 days + ₹2,000 second pamphlet drop.
  • Test a referral mechanic: "Send your friend a free raita — they get ₹30 off, you get ₹30 off."
  • Run one Swiggy/Zomato own-funded promo: 10 % off above ₹200, Friday–Sunday only.

Month 3 — find what scales

  • Goal: 25 orders/day, profitable on direct channels, breakeven on aggregators.
  • Budget: ₹10,000.
  • Pick one winning channel (likely WhatsApp + GBP) and double down.
  • Start collecting customer phone numbers in a Google Sheet → bulk WhatsApp Sunday-feast push every Saturday night.
  • Decide: do you need a part-time helper for delivery / roti-making? At 25 orders/day, yes.

Industry research — what successful cloud kitchens did right

Patterns from the kitchens that scaled in India. Worth re-reading every 30 days.

Rebel Foods (Behrouz, Faasos, Oven Story, etc.)

  • Started 2011 as Faasos — wraps. Realised single-brand cloud kitchen is hard to fill capacity.
  • Multi-brand model: one physical kitchen, 5–12 virtual brands on aggregators. Same chicken can become "biryani brand", "kathi roll brand", "bowl brand".
  • Lesson for you: in 6 months, consider listing as two brands on aggregators — "Desi Monk Kitchen" (curries) and a second virtual brand "Monk's Biryani" — same kitchen, double the search-result real estate.

Box8 (Eatfit umbrella)

  • IIT Bombay alums, focused on Indian thali / desi meals — exactly your category.
  • Standardised SKUs (every meal weighs the same), tight packaging, focused on office-lunch dabba market.
  • Lesson: standardisation beats variety. 10 plates of chicken curry should be indistinguishable from each other.

Biryani By Kilo

  • One hero SKU: handi biryani. Premium positioning.
  • Lesson: a single hero gets you discovery. Customers don't search "home food" — they search "chicken biryani near me". Pick a hero search term and own it.
  • Your hero candidates: "home-style chicken curry", "Sunday mutton feast".

The dabba originals (small operators, very relevant to you)

  • Most successful tiny home-kitchens in Bengaluru started by solving one specific person: the bachelor IT worker who wants ghar-jaisa khana within a 1-km radius. They didn't try to be everything.
  • Their pattern: 3 SKUs to launch. Subscriptions (15 meals / ₹1,800). 70 % repeat business by month 3.
  • Lesson: build a weekly subscription / dabba plan by month 4 — recurring revenue, no aggregator commission.

Common failure patterns to avoid

  • Menu bloat. Restaurants that launched with 30 items closed by month 6.
  • Aggregator dependence. Kitchens 100 % on Swiggy/Zomato die when commissions rise. Build direct from day one.
  • Founder cooks every meal. Doesn't scale past 25 orders/day. Plan a helper now.
  • Ignoring rating. A drop from 4.2 → 3.8 = 60 % traffic loss. Refund first, argue later.
  • Cash-flow blindness. Aggregator pays after 7–14 days. You need ₹50K–1L working capital float.

The repeat-customer obsession

A new customer costs ₹80 to acquire. A repeat customer costs ₹0. Every successful kitchen above has one rule: treat the first order like a wedding. Hand-written thank-you note, free raita, perfect packaging. The first order isn't profitable — it's marketing.

12

Risks & pitfalls

A kitchen doesn't usually die from one big problem — it dies from five small ones at once. Read this list weekly for the first 90 days.

Food-poisoning incident
Catastrophic
Cold chain, hand-wash, separate boards. Refrigerate within 2 hr. Don't reheat curry > once.
Rating drop < 4.0
Severe
Aggregator kills your discovery. Refund + apology + free dish to win back.
Supplier ghosting (mutton)
High
Always have 2 vendors. Map a 3-km backup plan in advance.
Cash flow gap (week 4)
High
Aggregator settles weekly with 7-day lag. Keep ₹50K–1L float.
Aggregator commission hike
Medium
Both platforms raise commission after 60 days. Direct channel must be growing by then.
Festival demand spike
Medium
Sunday + festivals = 3× orders. Pre-marinate Saturday afternoon. Cap orders per slot to avoid quality drop.
Power / gas outage during service
Medium
Backup gas cylinder always at home. Inverter for fridge during 1+ hr outages.
Rider not showing up (aggregator)
Low
Auto-handled by Swiggy/Zomato but slows your kitchen. Mark "ready" only when truly ready.
